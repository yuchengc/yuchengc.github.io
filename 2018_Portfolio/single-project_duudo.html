Duudo

AR-based social co-creation application

Have you ever tried to mark or to draw something that reflects your feelings or ideas at a specific location? Or, have you ever recalled an unforgettable memory when you visited an old place?

People’s memories have a high correlation with locations. They like to link the unforgettable things with the place they went and document that moment via media. People use postcard, doodle, photo or the other media to store their memory and they love to share with others who also have the same experience or idea. Exploring people’s thinking and responses is an interesting way for people to interact with each other.

For this project, we are passionate about transforming the way that people communicate their memory and ideas with friends and strangers. We want to continue the experience of these physical media yet in a digital way and provide a new approach for people to deliver and review other’s creations.

Design Process

UX Research

Pilot study
How do people share the special moments and ideas with people at a specific location?

The original goal for our team was to create a platform that connects people and exchanges their memory and the emotion of an important moment at a specific location. We started exploring all channels used for recording and sharing their idea and feeling and we learned the connection with the physical place. We studied college students around us and people on social media.

The goal of our study is

  • Understand the purposes that people record things physically and their emotions
  • Figure out the motivations of reviewing the contents recorded previously and changes in emotions
  • Reveal people’s opinions on “time gap” of messages
Interview

At the pilot study, we tried to understand the way that people leave and share the moment with friends and how do people react to these message through social approaches. We hold several interviews with people who like to travel or love to record their life and try to find the potential opportunities for us.


We focused on three types of activities

  • Sending postcard
  • Writing blog
  • Doodle

The interview questions include:

  • What made you decide to send/leave the message?
  • How were you feeling when you received/view this message?
  • Did you have experience reviewing the messages again after first receiving it?
  • What do you think about the differences between receiving messages physically (letters, postcards) and receiving the messages from instant message app?
Interview insights
People like to create graffitis to share their idea.

After studying these three behaviors, we specifically interested in doodle activity. Graffitis/doodles have its unique traits. It could not only send the message but also provide the visual surprise that encourages readers.

We were attracted by the way that graffitis groups people and transfers the message. We observed that people like to create graffitis every day privately or publicly. The graffiti surrounds us. Some of them symbolize memory for a certain period of time for a group of people; some are just random doodles that people made to record their lives; some are the responses for previous creations. All the graffitis pass the memorial moment and ideas to people.

Project Goal

Connect people via doodle
Our goal is creating a platform that connects people and shares their thinkings and creations at a memorable location.
How do people communicate through doodle?
Culture Probe

In order to understand how people communicate through doodle, we adopted the UX research method, culture probe. We posted 6 posters that have a large empty place and the instruction for UofM people. We also added few prompts on the poster to encourage people to draw on it. We posted it at 6 locations, vending machine area, student lounge, activity/ event area, and bus station. The goal for this culture probe study are:

  • Observe the contents left at public area
  • Discover the pattern of drawing
  • How do people interact with others’ creation
  • The added-on doodle

The duration of this culture probe study was 3 days. We kept tracking the poster and were inspired by the evolution of doodles. We collected it back at the end of the study and got total 4 posters after 3 days. We interpreted all the collected posters and found out some insights from the doodles.

From the posters, we discovered several interesting patterns and came out the design principles for the further design phase. However, we also had some questions for the doodlers. Thus, we hold the second interviews and invited the people who drew on our doodle posters.

We wanted to learn

  • What attracts participants to add to other people’s creation?
  • Will they like to know the evolution of their creation and how they track the add-on creations?
  • How frequently do they walk back to the same place and see the doodle?
  • How did they leave the doodle to their friends and what was their expectation of the doodle?
  • How did they invite friends to see the doodle and respond to them?

From the interviews, we learned

  • Users like to track and explore the evolution of the doodle work.
  • Users tend to react to friends’ creation and the fine doodle more than the strangers’ work.
  • Users want to receive notification regarding if other likes their creations or if anyone adds on their doodle but don’t want to be bothered by too many messages at the same time.
  • Doodles will be added and change gradually but users won’t be interested in every single update.
Design Principle

Based on the UX research and the observation, I transformed the insights into several principles that the design need to follow.

How to draw in AR environment

Why we adopted AR technology?

Since we like to connect people through digital doodle at the specific location, we need a media that could display the digital creations in the physical world. Augmented Reality is the perfect technology that could add the digital doodle in the real world. Without change the real environment, AR could create another channel that allows users to add their doodle and share with friends.

In order to learn how do people draw in AR environment, we studied several AR drawing apps and look into the interaction flow and the provided tools.

We focused on the following function: (1) Onboarding process and design, (2) Drawing tools, (3) Sharing approaches

Something we learned from these apps:

  • Most of the apps provide the onboarding prompts/instructions to teach users the way of drawing in AR.
  • Most of the apps cannot draw with friends together. They are lack of directed social interaction.
  • AR camera view is there default screen
  • Some apps just provide the basic tools for drawing; some apps have fancy brushes.
  • “Just a line” and “world brush” use Bluetooth and the Internet to group people together. They provide a real-time cooperation function for users to draw something.
  • Part of the apps provides a way to draw the doodle by phone’s movement.

Layout and UI Components Iteration

Floating tab bar


No.1 design proposal

This is the first design proposal from the teammate. The menu is designed on the bottom-left and all doodle tools are present as default on the right-hand side.

Pros:

  • Provide larger space for AR for more immersive experience.

Cons:

  • The menu is the hamburger menu. It is hard for users to identify all provided functions.
  • Doesn't separate the doodle mode and explore mode. Hard for users to switch the functions.

No.2 design proposal

The second design proposal tried to solve the menu problem. The major two functions, user profile, and collection are isolated into the homepage.

Pros:

  • Users could access the two major functions easily.

Cons:

  • Doesn't separate the doodle mode and explore mode. Hard for users to switch the functions.
No.3 design proposal

The third design proposal tried to solve the menu problem. As the project progresses, we find that users need more features. It is hard to display all functions on the home page. Thus, we tried the menu again.

Pros:

  • Use the entire display with only two control buttons.

Cons:

  • The additional step to access the functions.
  • Hamburger menu

No.4 design proposal

In order to letting users access the functions quickly, we tried the traditional tab bar.

Pros:

  • iOS users already familiar with this UI component. It matches users' expectation of switching function.

Cons:

  • According to iOS Augmented Reality Design Guide, the application should use the entire display. We should devote as much of the screen as possible for the immersive experience.

Final design decision

After considering the AR design guideline and the functions' hierarchy, I organized the structure again. The major three functions, doodle, user profile and collection, are selected to show on the tab bar. I also redesigned the tab bar. In order to show the AR view as many as possible, I transformed the bar as the floating bar. The floating bar doesn't block the whole bottom area. It provides more space to display the AR view and keeps the same functionality.

Timeline

I tried several ways to design the timeline for helping users exploring the doodle evolution. Start from the basic 2D UI to the AR integration. Below lists the iteration of the timeline design.

2D UI vs AR interaction

The first design of the timeline is the traditional 2D interface. I place the first doodle on the top and use the line to connect with the following doodle presenting the creation order. It is a safe design that could display the evolution information, but it lacks for the connection with the real doodle, the AR doodle. Thus, I start thinking about the possibility of showing the timeline in AR view.

To directly view the evolution in AR view, I design the scrollable timeline on the bottom of the screen. There are two designs of the AR timeline.

The first design: Slider

The timeline is a slider with several tick marks. Each tick mark represents a doodle. Users can move the slider thumb around each tick mark and view the change of the doodles wall. The slider is shorter than the screen width. Users can view all tick marks and predict the number of added-on doodles.


The final design

The final design continues the slider concept but transforms it a little bit. The thumb, the circle in the center, is not moveable now but the whole track, the bar behind the thumb, is moveable. Users can swipe the track behind and view each doodle's author. When the tick mark moves under the thumb, the doodle belong to that tick will show up and the author's picture will display on the thumb.

Final Design Features
1. Prime social features

Collaborative doodling

Add on others' creation via AR

When users discover inspirational creations, they like to provide their feedback and support to it.

Duudo provides the collaboration function for users to add-on or to draw anything they like. We create an environment for them to enjoy creating fantasy works together.

Direct access to liked doodles and my profile

Duudo connect people via the creations. Thus, helping users to access the created doodles and manage the works is very important.

We provide two major functions, user creation, and collection, for users to manage their work. We use the floating tab bar to present the major three functions of Duudo. Users can quickly access their creations and the creation they collected through the tab bar.

Highlight other’s updates on the doodle you’re following

In order to enhance the connection between doodlers, we add the tracking function for the doodle. If anyone adds on users' creations or the doodles they are following, the record will be updated simultaneously.

Research Insight: Doodler curious about what others have added to their creations. They also want to know if they get any comments or supports.

Private sharing

The doodle might be:

  • Something that user don’t like to share now
  • Personal memorial work
  • A draft for other
  • Completed doodle they want to show out

We provide the “private post” and "Public post" option for user. User could just save and post their work in private.


Some users tend to share the doodles only with their friends because they feel anxious about demonstrating their creations in public. Thus, we provide the “share link” function where users can directly connect with existing social media friends and share the link with a certain group of people.

Research insight:“Sometimes I just want to share this work with my best friends” “I don’t feel comfortable sharing my creations in public when I just randomly drew it”


Report spam and abuse

Considering the surveillance issue, we add the report function for users. We cooperate with users to maintain this co-creation world friendly and safe, preventing the abuse and inappropriate content from happening.

2. Motivate user to engage

Increase the visibility

We ask the users’ permission of their location and giving notification. By knowing their locations, we can give them notification actively whenever there is popular doodle nearby or some doodles are just being added.

This feature not only increases user engagement. Users will also feel more comfortable starting doodling when there’s already some doodles existing around their environment.

Research insight:

  • Giving the users notifications reminds them to get involved in Duudo more often.
  • Users are less likely to start doodling if they are the first creator in this environment.

Turn on camera automatically to streamline the exploration

Every time the user opens Duudo, the camera is automatically turned on for the users to explore the environment and doodles around them.

This decision is made to increase more engagement and discoverability where users feel more comfortable be parts of the co-creation and start doodling.

3. Communicative Graffiti in Digital

Timeline of doodle

Users can drag the timeline to see the evolution of doodles around them. Additionally, this feature makes our Duudo environment more socially engaged because everyone can trace the timeline of this environment as if they were here all the time.

Research insight:

  • What if there are too many doodles overlapping
  • Offer the chances to check the doodle evolvement
  • Some user cases who value their original creations a lot

Various AR doodling tools

Duudo provides different doodling tools for the user so that they can take the greatest advantage of this co-creation environment.


Research insight:

  • User need in terms of different tools or colors
  • Some similar patterns are detected across different cultural probe research (e.g. arrows and stickers)
4. Interaction with Physical Environment

Active Radar showing nearby doodles

The radar will light up when doodle is detected within 20m. Users will be motivated to explore the doodles nearby.

We make use of compass feature to create this active radar.

Display footprints on map

Icons are shown on map, to inform the location of:

  • Nearby doodle
  • Own creation
  • Liked creation

Larger icons are used to highlight unchecked updates

In the bottom, we show the total number of liked and owned creations, as well as visited places, to give users a sense of achievement in traveling.
