SmartCues

A multitouch interaction library based on D3 for details-on-demand through dynamically computed overlays.

Most of the data visualizations focus on desktop interaction and rely on the mouse and keyboard to inquire the detail. For these details-on-demand functions, they do not support touch interaction for the touch screen and only implemented with a constrained setting. However, it is a trend that more and more users use touch devices to access information and query the data. The SmartCues project was initiated by PhD student Hariharan Subramonyam and Prof. Eytan Adar at the University of Michigan. They proposed the touch interactions for three basic charts, bar, line, and scatter and they like to expend the possibility for different types of chart.

Date: 2018
My Role: D3 developor, Visualization prototyper
Teammates: Hariharan Subramonyam

In 2018, September, I join the team and closely work with Hariharan for the SmartCues library. I was in charge of adjusting and integrating the library for new types of chart and optimizing the interactions for these charts.

  • Bar
  • Pyramid
  • Bubble
  • Heatmap

Story line

To achieve the aforementioned goals, we divided the visualization into five parts. We use martini glass visualization structure to guide the audience.

1.Start with the high-level, current situation

Visualize current situation of undernourishment, which is a related concept that the public is more familiar to


Donut Chart

By showing the share of undernourished population around the world, the chart aims to give a general idea of global undernourishment and its distribution

    Encoding:
  • Color: severity of hunger

2.Introduce the Global hunger index, GHI

Visualize the definition of GHI

We explain the definition of GHI with static illustrations and short paragraphs.

3.Visualize the distribution of GHI values around the world

Map

The map indicates the global situation of GHI and allows viewers to have a general sense of spatial distribution from 1992 to 2018.


    Encoding:
  • Radius of circle: GHI index
  • Position of circle: geometric center of each country

4.Visualize the details of different related factors of GHI

Small Multiples

In order to present the deeper analysis of GHI, we use radar chart that represents each country for demonstrating the factors (Undernourishment rate, stunting rate, wasting rate, mortality rate, and underweight rate) of GHI.

    Encoding:
  • Color dot: real/simulated data
  • Background color: focus and select
  • Five axises: Undernourishment, Stunting,Wasting, Mortality, Underweight

5.Visualize the GDP and the factors of each country

Line & Bar Chart

The chart used to compare the trend of the four GHI indicators and GDP across the years. It also allows audiences to hide certain indicators.

As the end of the martini glass visualization structure, we also guide audience to interact with the chart and change the data range for further information.

    Encoding:
  • Color: different indicators
  • X axis: years
  • Left Y axis: GDP values
